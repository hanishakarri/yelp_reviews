all_reviews_text = ' '.join(yelp_df['text'].dropna())

words = all_reviews_text.split()

# Filter out common stop words and non-alphabetic tokens
stop_words = set(stopwords.words('english'))
filtered_words = [word.lower() for word in words if word.lower() not in stop_words and word.isalpha()]

# Create a DataFrame to count word occurrences
filtered_word_counts_df = pd.Series(filtered_words).value_counts().reset_index()
filtered_word_counts_df.columns = ['Word', 'Frequency']

# top most 15
top_words_df = filtered_word_counts_df.head(15)  


plt.figure(figsize=(12, 6))
plt.bar(top_words_df['Word'], top_words_df['Frequency'], color='green')
plt.title('Top 15 Most Common Words in Yelp Reviews',fontsize=12)
plt.xlabel('Words')
plt.ylabel('Frequency')
plt.xticks(rotation=0, ha='right')  

caption_text = "Note: Common stop words and non-alphabetic tokens are excluded from the analysis."
plt.text(0.5, -0.15, caption_text, ha='right', va='center', transform=plt.gca().transAxes, fontsize=8, color='black')


plt.show()

The visualization was designed to highlight the top 15 most common words in Yelp reviews while excluding common stop words and non-alphabetic tokens. The choice of a bar chart was made to effectively display the frequency of each word, providing a clear comparison. The color green was selected for a visually appealing and easily distinguishable representation. The title "Top 15 Most Common Words in Yelp Reviews" communicates the main focus of the chart. The font size of the title and axis labels was adjusted for readability, with a larger font size for the title for emphasis. The x-axis labels were rotated for better visibility, and the caption was added to convey the exclusion of certain words from the analysis. The placement of the caption at the bottom right ensures it does not interfere with the main content. The improved title includes a slightly larger font size and a specific mention of the excluded words, providing a more informative context for the audience.
